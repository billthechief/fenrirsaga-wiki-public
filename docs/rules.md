## 2. Gameplay Rules

### ğŸ Exploits and Bug Abuse
- Do not exploit bugs or abuse unintended game mechanics.
- If you find a potential exploit, you must report it in the `#bug-reports` channel or directly to a staff member, especially if it is sensitive in nature.

* **Penalty:** Deliberate abuse may result in temporary or permanent bans, subject to GM discretion.

### ğŸ’” Griefing & Mobbing

#### Mobbing Etiquette
- Mob trains are acceptable **only if** your party is alone on the map.
- If other players are present, be considerate. Communication solves most issues.
- While no one "owns" a map, intentionally disrupting another party's gameplay is considered griefing.

!!! note "Rule of Thumb"
    If someone is already on a map, reach out to them and find a way to share the space before you begin mobbing.

#### Kill & Loot Stealing
- Accidental Kill-Stealing (KS) happens. Please use the `@noks` command to prevent it.
- Repeated, intentional kill-stealing after a warning is considered griefing.
- Loot stealing is only permitted if the original killer gives you explicit permission.

* **Penalty:** Repeated offenses are considered griefing and are subject to bans.

### ğŸ† MVP Rules
MVPs are considered Free-for-All (FFA) with the following guidelines:

* The FFA rules only apply when the MVP is active and up for competition.
* These rules apply solely to players who are actively competing for the MVP.
* **Mob dropping** (using monsters that are not the MVP or its minions) or **dead branching** to interfere with another player's competition is strictly forbidden and considered griefing.

!!! question "What does 'Actively Competing' mean?"
    **You ARE actively competing if:**

    * Your full party, or a majority of it, is on the map and fighting the MVP.
    * Your party has engaged the MVP but died and is now returning to the fight.

    **You are NOT actively competing if:**

    * You cannot feasibly kill the MVP with your current party.
    * You have only scouted the MVP and your party is en-route (arriving in more than a minute).

!!! tip "Why This Matters"
    It can be difficult to distinguish between legitimate competition and griefing. To avoid frustration and potential penalties, arrive with your party **before** engaging an MVP and do not interrupt an ongoing fight unless you are clearly present and participating. Violations will be considered griefing.
